# MedicalLinks GCC Go-To-Market Plan

## 1) The business in one sentence
MedicalLinks helps hospitals across the GCC hire qualified doctors faster by combining trusted sourcing, careful screening, licensing-readiness checks, and managed placement.

## 2) The strategic choice
Do **not** launch as a broad job board first.

Launch as a **high-trust medical recruitment service with software support**:
- managed candidate intake
- hospital role intake
- credential and document screening
- shortlist creation
- interview coordination
- licensing and onboarding tracking
- placement follow-up

Why this is the right starting model:
- Hospitals do not mainly need more job listings. They need reliable doctors who can actually join.
- International doctors do not mainly need more job ads. They need a trusted route, clear expectations, and support through documentation and relocation.
- Your early advantage is relationships with hospitals and access to applicants, not mass traffic.

## 3) Market reality you should design around
In the GCC, the hiring problem is less about discovery and more about **execution**:
- matching the right specialty and grade
- verifying documents and references
- understanding licensing pathway by country
- confirming salary and package expectations
- managing relocation, notice period, and family logistics
- reducing drop-off between interview, offer, and arrival

Implication:
Your product should behave like a **placement workflow platform**, not a generic marketplace.

## 4) Initial target market

### Hospital-side ICP
Start with hospitals that have these traits:
- private hospitals or hospital groups that hire internationally
- hospitals with repeat physician recruitment needs
- hospitals that can move reasonably fast once profiles are shortlisted
- hospitals willing to share a real vacancy, compensation range, and hiring timeline
- hospitals that assign one accountable hiring contact

Good early targets:
- mid-sized private hospital groups
- specialty hospitals
- hospitals in Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman, and Bahrain with active physician hiring
- employers that already rely on expatriate medical talent

Avoid at first:
- hospitals with unclear approval chains
- employers who want unlimited CV access but low engagement
- clients who are highly price-sensitive but slow to act
- roles with unclear licensing feasibility

### Candidate-side ICP
Start with doctors who meet most of these conditions:
- already interested in GCC relocation
- qualified in a specialty or on a clear specialist path
- strong spoken English; Arabic is a plus for some markets
- credible, verifiable work history
- documents available or collectable
- realistic expectations on pay, housing, and package
- willingness to relocate within a defined timeline

Best initial wedge:
- doctors with recognized experience from countries and training systems already accepted by GCC employers
- specialties that are repeatedly hard to fill
- candidates with strong interview readiness and fast documentation turnaround

## 5) Positioning

### Core promise to hospitals
"We send you vetted doctors who are genuinely movable, credential-ready, and aligned on package, location, and timeline."

### Core promise to candidates
"A trusted path into GCC hospital roles — free for candidates, transparent, and guided from application to onboarding."

### What you are not
- not a mass CV database
- not only a job board
- not only a visa agency
- not only a licensing consultant

You are a **doctor recruitment and placement pipeline**.

## 6) Business model

## Recommended model: hybrid placement fee
Use:
- a small kickoff fee when a hospital opens a search
- a success fee when the candidate signs or starts, depending on the agreement
- an optional replacement guarantee
- optional paid add-ons for premium coordination

### Why hybrid is better than pure contingency
Pure contingency sounds attractive, but often creates:
- vague vacancies
- slow client feedback
- duplicated effort across too many low-priority roles
- too much unpaid work before commitment

A hybrid model filters for seriousness and improves cash flow.

## Recommended pilot pricing
Use these as testing ranges with the first 5 to 10 hospitals.

### Option A: flat fee by role level
- GP / junior doctor roles: USD 4k–8k equivalent
- specialist physician roles: USD 10k–18k equivalent
- consultant / senior consultant roles: USD 18k+

### Option B: hybrid
- kickoff fee: USD 1k–3k equivalent
- success fee on signing or start: remaining flat fee or 8%–15% of annual cash compensation
- replacement period: 60–120 days

Recommendation:
Start with **flat hybrid pricing**. It is easier to explain, easier to quote, and easier to compare internally.

## Candidate pricing
Keep candidate access free.
Optional paid services can come later:
- CV optimization
- interview coaching
- document organization help
- relocation planning support
- licensing guidance support

Do not charge for basic access to opportunities.

## 7) Service design

## Candidate funnel should have two stages

### Stage 1: Quick Apply
Goal: maximize qualified lead capture.
Time: 3 to 5 minutes.
Collect only what is needed to decide whether follow-up is worth it.

### Stage 2: Full Dossier
Goal: gather everything needed for matching and hiring execution.
Triggered only after screening.

This keeps candidate friction low while preserving data quality downstream.

## 8) Your wedge offer for launch
Offer hospitals three concrete things:

### A. Ready shortlist
Within a target SLA, send 3 to 5 screened doctor profiles for a live role.

### B. Credential clarity
Every profile should show:
- specialty and grade
- country of qualification
- years of experience
- current location
- language profile
- licensing status by target GCC market
- notice period
- relocation readiness
- missing documents

### C. Managed process
You coordinate:
- screening
- profile presentation
- interview scheduling
- feedback chasing
- reference and document collection
- offer tracking
- onboarding follow-up

That is where real value is created.

### Candidate offer

## 10) 90-day go-to-market plan

## Phase 0: Pre-launch (Weeks 1–2)
Objectives:
- validate the niche
- confirm pricing language
- prepare manual operating process

Tasks:
- interview 8 to 12 hospitals in your network
- interview 15 to 20 candidate-side contacts
- choose the initial country and specialty wedge
- define a shortlist standard
- define licensing-readiness rubric by GCC market
- rewrite intake flows
- prepare sales deck and hospital one-pager
- define role intake and candidate screening scripts

Deliverables:
- role intake template
- candidate screening rubric
- pilot pricing sheet
- shortlist profile template
- MVP backlog

## Phase 1: Concierge pilot (Weeks 3–8)
Objectives:
- place the first candidates manually
- learn bottlenecks before automating

Targets:
- 3 to 5 pilot hospitals
- 30 to 60 inbound or sourced candidates
- 10 to 20 fully screened candidates
- 5 to 10 live interviews
- 1 to 3 offers

How to work:
- run hospital intake manually
- screen candidates manually
- use a simple CRM or spreadsheet for matching
- track blocked steps by category: licensing, salary, timeline, documents, communication

## Phase 2: Productized operations (Weeks 9–12)
Objectives:
- move the highest-friction work into software
- make the process repeatable

Ship:
- quick apply form
- full dossier form
- admin dashboard
- role management
- shortlist tracking
- reminders and status changes
- bilingual content

## 11) Country strategy
Do **not** go multi-country in depth on day one.

Use a staged approach:
1. pick one primary GCC market for the first wave
2. support a second market only if the licensing and buyer behavior are already familiar
3. keep other GCC countries as expansion targets, not equal-priority markets

Suggested practical sequence:
- primary market: the country where your strongest hospital relationships and fastest deal cycles already exist
- secondary market: the one with similar client behavior and manageable complexity

## 12) Supply strategy
Candidate acquisition should come from multiple channels:
- founder network
- referrals from placed doctors
- targeted LinkedIn outreach
- WhatsApp and Telegram communities where relevant
- partnerships with training communities and recruiters in source markets
- content explaining GCC hiring pathways and expectations

Key principle:
Quality beats volume.
A smaller pool of well-screened doctors is more valuable than many weak applications.

## 13) Demand strategy
Hospital acquisition should be founder-led at first.

Use a simple outreach message:
- we specialize in physician recruitment
- we work on defined search mandates
- we screen for specialty fit, compensation alignment, documentation readiness, and relocation intent
- we reduce failed interviews and no-shows

Your early sales assets should be:
- one-page overview
- sample shortlist format
- pricing sheet
- candidate profile template
- replacement policy

## 14) Key operating metrics
Track these from the first week:

### Demand metrics
- number of active hospital clients
- number of live mandates
- average time from intake to shortlist
- hospital feedback turnaround time

### Supply metrics
- quick apply conversion rate
- screening-to-qualified rate
- qualified-to-interview rate
- interview-to-offer rate
- offer-to-start rate
- candidate drop-off reasons

### Quality metrics
- replacement rate
- 90-day retention rate
- client repeat rate
- referral rate from candidates and hospitals

## 15) Biggest risks and how to reduce them

### Risk 1: too many weak applicants
Response:
- use a short but selective quick apply
- add early knockout questions
- screen tightly

### Risk 2: hospital delays kill candidates
Response:
- only prioritize live, serious mandates
- set response expectations upfront
- pause low-engagement clients

### Risk 3: mismatch on package expectations
Response:
- collect compensation expectations early
- require clients to give package clarity before launch

### Risk 4: licensing complexity by country
Response:
- build a country-by-country internal checklist
- do not promise timelines you cannot control

### Risk 5: candidate drop-off after offer
Response:
- track motivation and family constraints early
- keep close follow-up through joining
- provide clear onboarding support

## 16) The core recommendation
Build MedicalLinks first as a **managed GCC doctor placement business with software support**, not as a self-serve marketplace.

Win by being:
- faster than generic recruiters
- more credible than job boards
- more organized than informal brokers
- more candidate-friendly than transactional agencies
